# Olist E-Commerce Analysis 🛍️

Brazilian e-commerce is fascinating - and messy. I took Olist's 
public dataset (96K+ orders) and dug into what's actually driving 
sales, delays, and customer satisfaction through exploratory data analysis in Jupyter notebook.

## What I looked at
- **Sales trends** — how revenue changed month by month (spoiler: 
  there's a clear growth story with a few interesting dips)
- **Customer behavior** — LTV distribution, new vs returning buyers
- **Delivery performance** — who's late, by how much, and does it matter
- **Reviews** — turns out late deliveries really do tank your rating

## What I found
- Average order value is ~$205, but the median is ~$109 — 
  a small group of large orders is pulling the mean up significantly
- **8.1% of orders arrive late** — that's 1 in 12 customers 
  getting a worse experience than promised
- Late deliveries directly correlate with lower review scores — 
  fixing logistics isn't just operational, it's a retention issue
- The top 10% of customers spend 2x the average — 
  clear segmentation opportunity

## Tools
Jupyter · Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) · Tableau · SQL
